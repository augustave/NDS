# NDS Knowledge Base

## Core Thesis

The National Design Studio represents a centralized attempt to modernize the digital and physical interface of the U.S. federal government. Its design logic combines federal service modernization, private-sector product velocity, national brand architecture, AI-assisted deployment, and physical-digital service integration.

## Primary Entities

### America by Design

A federal initiative launched through Executive Order 14338, "Improving Our Nation Through Better Design," signed on August 21, 2025. It directs agencies to improve the visual presentation and usability of federal services across digital and physical environments.

### National Design Studio

A temporary organization inside the Executive Office of the President. It is tasked with guiding agencies toward better design, lower duplicated design cost, standardized design systems, and visible modernization results by July 4, 2026.

### Chief Design Officer

The role held by Joe Gebbia. The Chief Design Officer leads the design transformation mandate, recruits talent, advises agencies, modernizes digital services, and connects the digital government experience to physical service environments.

### Chief Brand Architect

A brand leadership role associated with creating a unified federal visual and experiential system. The mandate is not only better websites, but a coherent national service identity.

## Operating Mandate

The NDS mandate includes:

• Improve usability and aesthetics of federal digital services.

• Improve physical service environments, not just websites.

• Reduce duplicated design cost through standardized design systems.

• Create initial visible results by July 4, 2026.

• Operate with urgency as a time-boxed organization.

• Align agencies around a more unified "One Government" experience.

## Baseline Problem

The pre-NDS federal interface is fragmented:

• Approximately 27,000 federal domains and subdomains.

• Decentralized procurement and agency-by-agency design choices.

• Low adoption of the U.S. Web Design System.

• Inconsistent mobile performance.

• Confusing, dated, and uneven civic service interfaces.

• Poor translation between digital workflows and physical service spaces.

## Joe Gebbia Role Model

The role model is not "generic designer." It is a public-sector Chief Design Officer with private-sector product instincts.

### Core Posture

• Treat federal services as real products used by millions of people.

• Reduce friction aggressively.

• Make interfaces beautiful, fast, legible, and coherent.

• Treat citizen dignity as a service-design outcome.

• Push agencies toward shared systems rather than custom local reinvention.

• Use the July 4, 2026 milestone as a forcing function.

### Design Philosophy

The design philosophy can be summarized as:

> Government services should feel modern, coherent, easy to use, and worthy of national confidence.

This means balancing:

• Delight with seriousness.

• Standardization with accessibility.

• Speed with legal compliance.

• Brand coherence with civic neutrality.

• AI acceleration with human accountability.

## Strategic Tensions

The skill should always notice these tensions. Each includes a *watch indicator* — a concrete signal the agent should look for in any service review.

### Speed vs. Compliance

Rapid deployment can produce accessibility and legal risk. Public services cannot behave like consumer beta software.

*Watch indicator:* a launch date set before the accessibility, privacy, or legal review has scheduled completion.

### Brand vs. Democracy

A unified government brand may build trust, but it can also make citizenship feel like a consumer transaction.

*Watch indicator:* marketing-style language ("welcome home," "your journey," celebratory imagery) appearing in benefit-eligibility or enforcement flows.

### AI Acceleration vs. Institutional Memory

AI-assisted code generation and private-sector talent can move quickly, but agencies may lose internal capability if knowledge is not transferred.

*Watch indicator:* an AI-generated or vendor-built component with no agency-side maintainer named in the handoff.

### Aesthetic Authority vs. Local Context

Centralized design systems reduce chaos, but some agencies serve highly specific populations whose needs cannot be flattened into a single visual style.

*Watch indicator:* a population-specific need (tribal nations, rural broadband-limited users, multilingual coastal communities, disability-specific service lines) being deferred to "phase 2" or removed for brand consistency.

### Private-Sector Talent vs. Conflict of Interest

TechForce-style talent pipelines may solve capability gaps while introducing ethics, procurement, and regulatory-capture concerns.

*Watch indicator:* the vendor or contractor advising on a procurement is in line to win the resulting contract.

## Evaluation Criteria

A good NDS-style recommendation should be judged by:

• User clarity
• Accessibility
• Mobile performance
• Reduced steps
• Reduced duplicated design cost
• Cross-agency consistency
• Lower service burden
• Evidence of measurable improvement
• Civic dignity
• Legal and ethical defensibility

## Red Flags

The skill should flag:

• Design that looks polished but fails accessibility.

• AI-generated code without semantic review.

• Nationalistic branding that overwhelms neutral public service goals.

• Interfaces that assume high bandwidth or high digital literacy.

• Centralized standards that erase agency-specific service needs.

• Physical redesigns that ignore actual frontline workflows.

• Service simplification that removes necessary legal, security, or anti-fraud friction.

## Cited Legal Frameworks

These are the statutory and executive instruments the skill should reference when grounding accessibility, plain-language, and modernization claims. The skill is not a substitute for legal counsel, but it must name the relevant framework rather than gesture vaguely at "compliance."

• **Executive Order 14338 — "Improving Our Nation Through Better Design"** (signed August 21, 2025). The originating mandate for America by Design and the National Design Studio. Cite when explaining the source of authority.

• **Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act** (as amended; current standard is the 2018 ICT Refresh harmonized with WCAG 2.0 AA). Federal accessibility floor for digital content and ICT. Cite when defining the accessibility bar.

• **Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973**. Broader non-discrimination obligation against disability-based exclusion in federally funded programs. Cite when accessibility intersects with service eligibility or physical access.

• **21st Century Integrated Digital Experience Act (21st Century IDEA Act, 2018)**. Requires agencies to modernize websites, digitize forms, accept electronic signatures, and meet usability and accessibility standards. Cite when justifying form-digitization or website-modernization scope.

• **Plain Writing Act of 2010**. Requires federal agencies to use clear writing in covered documents. Cite when recommending content rewrites or plain-language audits.

• **U.S. Web Design System (USWDS)**. Not a statute, but the canonical federal design system endorsed for compliance and consistency. Cite as the default component and pattern library.

## Measurement Baselines

A redesign without measurable baselines is theater. The skill should attempt to capture or estimate the following for any service it touches, even when only directional estimates are possible.

• **Task completion rate** — percentage of users who successfully complete the primary task (file a claim, pay a balance, check status) per session.

• **Time-to-completion** — median minutes from arrival to confirmation, broken out for first-time vs. returning users.

• **Error and recovery rate** — percentage of submissions that hit a validation error, and what percentage of those recover vs. abandon.

• **Mobile completion percentage** — share of completions originating on mobile devices; mobile-vs-desktop gap is a quality indicator.

• **Support-call volume** — calls per 1,000 service interactions, with topic breakdown if available. Disproportionate volume on a single topic is a friction signal.

• **Abandoned-form rate** — percentage of started forms that are never submitted.

• **Accessibility audit score** — number of WCAG 2.0 AA / Section 508 issues identified per manual + automated audit pass.

• **Duplicated-design-cost estimate** — number of custom interface patterns where a USWDS pattern would have sufficed; rough cost of redundant component maintenance.

• **Cross-agency consistency score** — qualitative or rubric-based score of how recognizable navigation, form patterns, and confirmation states are across agencies a typical user touches in a calendar year.

• **Civic dignity indicator** — qualitative signal: does the service preserve user dignity at moments of stress? Captured through user research, not metrics alone.

## Canonical Service Archetypes

These archetypes anchor the skill's reasoning. When the agent is asked to "modernize a federal service" without specifying which, it should default to recognizing which archetype the service most resembles.

### SSA Retirement Claim
• Primary user stress: end-of-career, fixed-income anxiety, irreversibility of choices.
• Common failure: confusing eligibility logic, intimidating PDF forms, opaque processing-time messaging.
• Physical touchpoint: SSA field office; long waits, paper printouts, staff-mediated form completion.

### IRS Payment Portal
• Primary user stress: financial liability, fear of penalty, identity-verification anxiety.
• Common failure: authentication flow drop-off, unclear payment-plan eligibility, status messages indistinguishable from errors.
• Physical touchpoint: IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center; appointment-only, paper-heavy.

### USCIS Status Check
• Primary user stress: immigration limbo, legal stakes, language-access gap.
• Common failure: case-status codes that require external decoding, no time-bound expectations, inconsistent notification channels.
• Physical touchpoint: USCIS field office and ASC; biometrics appointments, document handling.

### Passport Renewal
• Primary user stress: travel-date pressure, document-fragility anxiety, fee opacity.
• Common failure: photo-validation rejections, address-change handling, expedite vs. standard confusion.
• Physical touchpoint: passport-acceptance facility (often a post office) and regional passport agency for in-person.

### VA Benefits
• Primary user stress: service-connected trauma, claim-denial history, distrust of the agency.
• Common failure: redirect mazes between VA.gov, VA Health, and benefits portals; inconsistent identity systems; appeals-process opacity.
• Physical touchpoint: VA medical center, regional benefits office, vet center; uneven staff training.

### Disaster Recovery (FEMA)
• Primary user stress: acute loss, displacement, low-bandwidth or no-power environment.
• Common failure: registration flow assumes broadband and stable address; reliance on document upload when documents were destroyed; ineligibility messaging that reads as accusation.
• Physical touchpoint: Disaster Recovery Center (mobile/temporary), shelter intake, mobile registration.

## AI Use Boundaries

This is the canonical boundary set that SKILL.md and SWARM.md defer to. AI is acceleration; it is not authority.

### Allowed (AI-led, human-spot-checked)
• Inventorying pages, components, forms, and content across a domain.
• First-pass content summarization or simplification (must be plain-language reviewed before publishing).
• Migration of legacy markup into design-system components, with human review of semantic correctness.
• Generation of test cases, draft analytics queries, draft service blueprints.
• Pattern discovery: clustering similar forms or finding duplicate UI components across agencies.

### Supervised (AI-assisted, human-of-record)
• Drafting public-facing copy, error messages, and confirmation text — requires plain-language and legal review.
• Producing service blueprints, friction maps, and risk registers — requires human sign-off before circulation.
• Accessibility audits — automated tooling is a floor; manual review by an accessibility specialist is required.
• Code generation for citizen-facing interfaces — requires human security and semantic review.

### Prohibited (AI must not be the authority)
• Final determinations on benefit eligibility, tax liability, immigration status, or any individualized statutory adjudication.
• Final accessibility conformance sign-off.
• Authoritative legal language in citizen-facing materials.
• Safety-critical instructions (medical, emergency, evacuation).
• Personally identifying information processing without explicit privacy-impact assessment.
• Replacing human escalation paths. Every AI-mediated service must offer a human alternative.

## Glossary

• **USWDS** — U.S. Web Design System. The federal government's open-source design system and pattern library, hosted at designsystem.digital.gov.
• **NDS** — National Design Studio. The temporary organization inside the Executive Office of the President established under EO 14338.
• **CDO** — Chief Design Officer. The role held by Joe Gebbia, leading NDS.
• **CBA** — Chief Brand Architect. Peer leadership role responsible for unified federal visual and experiential identity.
• **EO 14338** — Executive Order "Improving Our Nation Through Better Design," signed August 21, 2025.
• **TechForce** — Shorthand for the private-sector technical-talent pipeline being routed into federal modernization work. Source of both capability and conflict-of-interest concerns.
• **One Government** — The strategic objective of presenting a coherent cross-agency experience to the public, rather than 27,000 separate agency identities.
• **21st Century IDEA Act** — Federal law requiring agency websites, forms, and digital services to meet modern usability, accessibility, and digitization standards.
• **Section 508** — Federal accessibility standard for ICT, currently harmonized with WCAG 2.0 AA.
• **WCAG** — Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. The international accessibility standard maintained by the W3C; Section 508 incorporates WCAG 2.0 AA.
